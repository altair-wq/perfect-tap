# PM Workstream — What Your PM Owns

You're vibe coding. Your PM runs everything else in parallel. Here's the split.

## Before Day 1 (start TODAY, lead time matters)
- [ ] Apple Developer Program enrollment ($99/yr)
- [ ] Google Play Console enrollment ($25 one-time)
- [ ] AppLovin account (free, even if we use AdMob first — keeps option open)
- [ ] Domain purchase (any cheap .com / .app)
- [ ] Privacy policy generated and hosted on the domain
- [ ] App name brainstorm — 10 candidates, check both store availability
- [ ] Mac access secured (own, borrow, or MacInCloud subscription)

## During Day 1
- [ ] App icon design — Figma, Canva, or buy on Iconscout (~$5)
- [ ] Competitor teardown: install 5 top tap-timing games, write 1-page "what they do well / what they suck at" doc
- [ ] Color palette decided (right now it's #00d4ff / #0a0e1a — change if needed)
- [ ] First screenshot mockups in Figma using prototype screenshots

## During Day 2
- [ ] Store listing copy finalized (use templates in `store-listing.md`)
- [ ] All screenshots produced at required sizes for both platforms
- [ ] Privacy nutrition labels filled in App Store Connect
- [ ] Play Console questionnaires answered (content rating, target audience, ads, data safety)
- [ ] AdMob ad units created, IDs handed to dev
- [ ] Support email set up (a Gmail is fine for v1)

## Post-launch (Days 3–14)
- [ ] Daily check of crash reports
- [ ] Daily check of install numbers + ad revenue
- [ ] Read every review, reply to every review (this matters for ranking)
- [ ] Track key metrics:
  - Day 1 retention (% of installs who open the app the next day)
  - Average session length
  - Games per session
  - Revenue per DAU
- [ ] Write post-mortem at day 14: what worked, what didn't, what's next

## Post-mortem template
1. What was the goal? (Learn end-to-end shipping)
2. Did we hit it? Why or why not?
3. Top 3 things that took longer than expected
4. Top 3 things that were faster than expected
5. What we'd change about the process next ship
6. What we'd change about the product
7. Decision: next game, or v1.1 of this one?
